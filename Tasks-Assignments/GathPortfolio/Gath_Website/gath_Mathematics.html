Euler's formula states that:

e ix = cos x + i sin x, ∀ x ∈ ℝ, where e is the exponential constant and i 2 = − 1.

However, as π ∈ ℝ is a particular real number case we can substitute it in the place of x, giving:

e iπ = cos π + i sin π.

Further, since cos π = − 1 and sin π = 0 then it follows that:

e iπ = − 1,

which leads to Euler's identity:

e iπ + 1 = 0 ∎

This amazing identity is so profound that it is simply not fully comprehended. It includes the additive identity (zero - where any number added to it remains unchanged) and the multiplitive identity (one - where any number multiplied by it remains unchanged). It also includes the transdental real numbers π and e, neither of which can be defined as any form of fraction and remain constant (π [3.14159... to 5 decimal places] being the constant ratio between a circle's radius and its circumference or area and e [2.71828... to 5 decimal places] being the exponential constant that is frequently used to model physical and economic growth). Additionally, it includes the remarkable invention of the imaginary number i, which is the square root of − 1. Although this number is imaginary it has proved to provide real world solutions in describing the propogation of electro-magnetic waves (like radio and ultra-sonic waves for example).
